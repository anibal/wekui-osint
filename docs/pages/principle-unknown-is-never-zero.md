title:: An unknown is never written down as zero
type:: principle

- When we do not know a value, we record that we do not know it. A blank is a statement about our knowledge; a zero is a claim about the world, and the two must never be confused.
- Where it already operates, verbatim:
  - [[query]] — "A Query that ran before we started counting has neither: something unknown is never written down as zero."
  - [[no-place]] — *not yet examined* is never collapsed into *examined and empty*: "If the two collapse into one, we can never tell *not yet examined* from *examined and empty*, and we cannot measure how well any Actor does the placing."
  - [[unplaced-place]] — Unplaced is an open question, not an answer: "a waiting room, not a location."
